# Outreach Templates Reference

KOINO Capital Original IP. Frameworks, not scripts. Adapt every template to the prospect.

## Cold Email Frameworks

### Framework 1: The Observation Opener
```
Subject: [specific thing you noticed about their business]

Hey [First Name],

Noticed [specific observation — e.g., "your team just opened a Denver office" or "you posted about struggling with X"].

We helped [similar company] solve that exact problem — they went from [before metric] to [after metric] in [timeframe].

Would it make sense to share how? Either way, congrats on [the thing you observed].

[Your name]
```

**Why it works:** Opens with proof you did research. Delivers social proof. Low-pressure CTA. Closes with genuine goodwill.

### Framework 2: The Value-First
```
Subject: [resource] for [their company]

[First Name],

Put together a quick [audit/analysis/list/teardown] of [their specific thing — website, funnel, content, ads].

Three things jumped out:
1. [Specific finding #1]
2. [Specific finding #2]
3. [Specific finding #3]

Happy to walk through it if useful. No pitch, just thought it might help.

[Your name]
```

**Why it works:** Leads with free value. Shows competence through specifics. "No pitch" disarms resistance.

### Framework 3: The Mutual Connection
```
Subject: [Mutual connection name] suggested I reach out

Hi [First Name],

[Mutual connection] mentioned you might be looking at [problem area]. We just wrapped a project with [similar company] that [specific result].

Worth a 15-minute call to see if there's a fit? If not, no worries at all.

[Your name]
```

### Framework 4: The Breakup (Final Follow-Up)
```
Subject: closing the loop

[First Name],

Sent a couple notes over the past few weeks about [topic].

Totally understand if the timing isn't right. I'll close this thread out, but if [problem] becomes a priority down the road, I'm easy to find.

All the best,
[Your name]
```

**Why it works:** Removes pressure. Triggers loss aversion. Gets more replies than any other email in the sequence.

## LinkedIn Frameworks

### Connection Request (280 char limit)
```
[First Name] — saw your [specific post/talk/article about X]. Really resonated with the point about [specific detail]. Would love to connect.
```

**Rules:**
- NEVER pitch in the connection request
- Reference something specific they created or said
- Keep it under 280 characters
- No "I'd love to add you to my network" (default LinkedIn garbage)

### Post-Connection DM Sequence

**DM #1 (after they accept, same day):**
```
Thanks for connecting, [First Name]. Really enjoyed your take on [specific topic from their content]. Curious — are you finding that [related question about their industry/challenge]?
```

**DM #2 (3-5 days later, only if they respond):**
```
That makes sense. We've been seeing the same thing with [type of companies]. One thing that's worked for [similar company]: [specific tactic/insight]. Thought you might find that useful.
```

**DM #3 (only if conversation is warm):**
```
[First Name], based on what you've shared — would it make sense to jump on a quick call? I have some ideas specific to [their situation] that might be worth exploring. Either way, appreciate the conversation.
```

### LinkedIn Comment Strategy (Pre-Outreach Warmup)
Before sending a connection request, engage with their content for 1-2 weeks:
1. Like 2-3 of their posts
2. Leave 1-2 thoughtful comments (NOT "Great post!" — add a genuine insight or question)
3. Share one of their posts with your own commentary
4. THEN send the connection request — they'll recognize your name

## Instagram/Twitter DM Framework
```
Hey [First Name] — just saw your [specific post/story about X]. [One sentence of genuine reaction]. Quick question: [relevant question about their business challenge]?
```

**Rules:**
- Only DM if they're active on the platform (posted in last 7 days)
- Reference a SPECIFIC post, not generic praise
- Ask a question, don't pitch
- Max 3 sentences
- If they don't respond to 1 DM, do NOT follow up on this channel

## Subject Line Vault

**Proven performers (4-7 words, no clickbait):**
- `[their company] + [your company]`
- `quick question about [specific topic]`
- `idea for [their specific initiative]`
- `[mutual connection] suggested I write`
- `[their company]'s [specific metric/page/campaign]`
- `congrats on [specific milestone]`
- `thought on [their recent content]`
- `noticed something on [their site/profile]`

**Avoid:**
- ALL CAPS anything
- Emojis in subject lines for cold email
- "Quick question" without specificity
- "Partnership opportunity"
- "Can I get 15 minutes?"
- Anything that sounds like a newsletter

## Personalization Research Checklist

Before writing any outreach, find at least 3 of these:

- [ ] Their most recent LinkedIn post or article
- [ ] A recent company milestone (funding, hire, launch, award)
- [ ] Their tech stack (BuiltWith, Wappalyzer)
- [ ] A specific page on their website that relates to your service
- [ ] Their hiring patterns (what roles = what problems)
- [ ] A mutual connection or shared community
- [ ] Their competitor and how your prospect differs
- [ ] A public statement they've made (podcast, interview, tweet)
- [ ] Their business model and likely pain points at their stage
- [ ] Recent reviews or customer feedback about them

**Rule:** If you can't find 3 hooks, they're either not a good prospect or you haven't researched enough. Never send generic outreach.

## Deliverability Checklist (Pre-Campaign)

- [ ] SPF record configured
- [ ] DKIM record configured
- [ ] DMARC policy set (at minimum p=none for monitoring)
- [ ] Email accounts warmed for 14+ days
- [ ] Sending domain is NOT your primary business domain (use a secondary)
- [ ] List verified through ZeroBounce/NeverBounce (<2% bounce target)
- [ ] No links in first email
- [ ] No attachments in any cold email
- [ ] No HTML — plain text only
- [ ] Sending volume: max 50/inbox/day
- [ ] 3-5 mailbox rotation configured
- [ ] Unsubscribe mechanism present (CAN-SPAM compliance)
- [ ] Physical address in signature (CAN-SPAM compliance)
