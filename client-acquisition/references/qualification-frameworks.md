# Qualification Frameworks Reference

KOINO Capital Original IP. Score every lead before spending real time on them.

---

## BANT Framework (Foundation)

Score 0-25 on each dimension. Total 0-100.

### Budget (0-25)
| Score | Signal |
|-------|--------|
| 0-5 | No budget, no history of paying for services, bootstrapped with no revenue |
| 6-10 | Has budget but unclear amount, or budget is below your minimum |
| 11-15 | Budget exists, roughly in your range, but not confirmed |
| 16-20 | Budget confirmed, in your range, has paid for similar services before |
| 21-25 | Budget exceeds your price, actively allocating spend, money is not the blocker |

**Discovery questions:**
- "What have you invested in [this area] before?"
- "Do you have a budget allocated for this, or would we need to build the case?"
- "What would solving this problem be worth to you annually?"

### Authority (0-25)
| Score | Signal |
|-------|--------|
| 0-5 | Talking to someone with no buying power, can't even recommend |
| 6-10 | Influencer but not decision-maker, unclear org structure |
| 11-15 | Has influence, knows the decision-maker, can make introduction |
| 16-20 | Decision-maker for this type of purchase, one approval above |
| 21-25 | THE decision-maker, signs contracts, controls budget |

**Discovery questions:**
- "Walk me through how your team has made decisions like this before."
- "Besides yourself, who else would need to be involved in evaluating this?"
- "If we both agreed this was a fit, what would the next steps look like on your end?"

### Need (0-25)
| Score | Signal |
|-------|--------|
| 0-5 | No real pain, browsing, "just exploring" |
| 6-10 | Mild pain but not urgent, could live with status quo |
| 11-15 | Real pain acknowledged, actively thinking about solutions |
| 16-20 | Urgent pain, actively evaluating solutions, deadline approaching |
| 21-25 | Hair-on-fire problem, failing without a solution, every day costs money |

**Discovery questions:**
- "What happens if you don't solve this in the next 90 days?"
- "How is this problem affecting your [revenue/team/customers] right now?"
- "What have you already tried?"

### Timeline (0-25)
| Score | Signal |
|-------|--------|
| 0-5 | "Maybe next year," no urgency, no forcing event |
| 6-10 | "Sometime this quarter," vague but interested |
| 11-15 | "Next month," has a rough timeline |
| 16-20 | "Within 2 weeks," has a deadline or forcing event |
| 21-25 | "Yesterday," contract ready, board pressure, competitive threat |

**Discovery questions:**
- "Is there a specific date or event driving the timeline?"
- "When would you ideally have this solved by?"
- "What would need to be true for you to make a decision this month?"

## MEDDIC Framework (For Deals >$10K)

Use MEDDIC as an overlay on BANT for complex, high-value sales.

### Metrics
**Question:** What specific, measurable outcome does the prospect expect?

| Strength | Signal |
|----------|--------|
| Weak | "We want to grow" (vague, no numbers) |
| Medium | "We want 20% more leads" (directional) |
| Strong | "We need 50 qualified meetings/month at <$200 CAC by Q3" (specific, time-bound) |

**Action:** If metrics are vague, help them define success. The deal is stronger when YOU help set the goalposts.

### Economic Buyer
**Question:** Who actually signs the check?

Map the buying committee:
- **Economic Buyer:** Controls budget, signs contract
- **Technical Buyer:** Evaluates capabilities, integration, security
- **User Buyer:** Will use the solution day-to-day
- **Coach/Champion:** Wants you to win, guides you through the org

**Red flag:** If you've never spoken to the Economic Buyer, your deal is at risk. Champions get overruled.

### Decision Criteria
**Question:** How will they compare options and make a final decision?

Common criteria to uncover:
- Price vs. value weighting
- Industry experience requirements
- Technical requirements (integrations, security, compliance)
- Reference requirements
- Team size / capacity concerns
- Speed of implementation

**Action:** If you don't know their decision criteria, you can't position against competitors. Ask directly: "What will ultimately determine which direction you go?"

### Decision Process
**Question:** What are the literal steps from "we like this" to "contract signed"?

Map every step:
1. Who needs to approve?
2. Is there a legal review?
3. Procurement process?
4. Board approval needed?
5. What's the typical cycle time?
6. Are they evaluating other vendors?

**Red flag:** If the prospect can't articulate their decision process, they may not be a real buyer.

### Identify Pain
**Question:** What is the cost of NOT solving this problem?

Quantify pain in three dimensions:
- **Financial:** Revenue lost, costs incurred, efficiency lost
- **Emotional:** Stress, frustration, embarrassment, fear
- **Strategic:** Competitive disadvantage, missed market window, talent loss

**The pain formula:**
```
Cost of Inaction = (Monthly cost of the problem) x (Months until they solve it without you)
```

If Cost of Inaction < Your Price, the deal is dead. Help them see the real cost.

### Champion
**Question:** Do you have someone inside the org actively selling for you when you're not in the room?

| Champion Strength | Signal |
|-------------------|--------|
| None | No internal advocate |
| Weak | Likes you but won't stick their neck out |
| Medium | Advocates for you, shares info, but limited influence |
| Strong | Has influence, shares intel, coaches you on objections, personally invested in your success |

**Action:** If you don't have a champion, you're selling blind. Find one or create one by solving a small problem for someone inside.

## Combined Scoring Matrix

```
BANT Score: ___/100
MEDDIC Score: ___/100 (if applicable)
Combined Score: (BANT x 0.6) + (MEDDIC x 0.4) = ___/100

90-100: HOT — Propose immediately, fast-track closing
70-89:  WARM — Strong prospect, address gaps, move to proposal
50-69:  LUKEWARM — Needs nurturing, qualify further, don't propose yet
30-49:  COOL — Long-term nurture, check back quarterly
0-29:   DEAD — Disqualify, don't waste time, move on
```

## Disqualification Signals (Kill the Deal Fast)

- They want a "free trial" or "proof of concept" before any commitment
- They can't articulate the problem they're solving
- The "decision-maker" keeps adding new decision-makers
- They're comparing you to DIY or hiring an internal person
- Timeline is "whenever" with no forcing event
- They've ghosted after receiving your proposal (follow up once, then move on)
- They negotiate price before understanding scope
- They bad-mouth their previous vendor excessively (you'll be next)
- They want to "think about it" after the third meeting

## Objection Pre-Handle Matrix

| Objection | Root Cause | Pre-Handle in Nurture |
|-----------|-----------|----------------------|
| "Too expensive" | Haven't felt the pain deeply enough | Share cost-of-inaction calculator before pricing |
| "Bad timing" | No forcing event | Share competitor moves or market shifts creating urgency |
| "Need to think about it" | Unresolved concern they won't voice | Address top 3 objections proactively in your proposal |
| "We're talking to others" | Normal — they should be | Share differentiation and case studies early |
| "We tried something similar" | Past failure creating risk aversion | Lead with how your approach differs and why previous approaches fail |
| "I need to check with my team" | Not the decision-maker, or need internal buy-in | Provide a one-pager they can share internally |
| "Can you do it cheaper?" | Price anchoring or genuinely budget-constrained | Offer scope adjustment, not discount — "We can do X at that price" |
