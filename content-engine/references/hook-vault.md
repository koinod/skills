# Hook Vault — 50+ Proven Hook Formulas

> Every formula includes the structure, why it works psychologically, and an example.
> Apply these to extracted content moments. Never post without a hook.

---

## Category 1: CURIOSITY GAP (The brain cannot resist an open loop)

### 1. The Unfinished Story
**Formula:** "I was about to [action] when [unexpected thing happened]..."
**Why:** Open loop creates tension. Viewer must watch to close it.
**Example:** "I was about to sign the biggest deal of my career when the client said three words that changed everything..."

### 2. The Hidden Truth
**Formula:** "Nobody talks about [specific thing] in [industry/topic]..."
**Why:** Implies insider knowledge. Viewer feels they're getting exclusive access.
**Example:** "Nobody talks about the real reason 90% of content strategies fail..."

### 3. The Number Reveal
**Formula:** "[Specific number] [things] that [unexpected outcome]"
**Why:** Specificity creates credibility. Numbers promise structured value.
**Example:** "3 caption mistakes that are killing your engagement right now"

### 4. The "After X Years"
**Formula:** "After [years/attempts/failures], here's what actually [works/matters]..."
**Why:** Implies earned wisdom. Positions speaker as battle-tested.
**Example:** "After 8 years of building businesses, here's the one thing that actually moves the needle..."

### 5. The Forbidden Knowledge
**Formula:** "[Authority/industry] doesn't want you to know this..."
**Why:** Creates us-vs-them dynamic. Viewer feels rebellious watching.
**Example:** "Marketing agencies don't want you to know this about their pricing..."

## Category 2: CONTROVERSY / CONTRARIAN (Disagreement drives comments)

### 6. The Hot Take
**Formula:** "[Popular belief] is completely wrong. Here's why..."
**Why:** Challenges identity. People MUST comment to defend or agree.
**Example:** "Posting every day is completely wrong. Here's why..."

### 7. The "Stop Doing This"
**Formula:** "Stop [common practice] immediately. It's [destroying/costing] your [thing]."
**Why:** Creates urgency + fear of loss (stronger motivator than gain).
**Example:** "Stop using trending audio immediately. It's destroying your reach."

### 8. The Unpopular Opinion
**Formula:** "Unpopular opinion: [bold statement that 40% agree with]"
**Why:** The sweet spot is 40/60 split — enough agreement to get shares, enough disagreement to get comments.
**Example:** "Unpopular opinion: you don't need a content strategy. You need a content habit."

### 9. The "Everyone Is Wrong"
**Formula:** "99% of [people] get [topic] wrong. Here's the 1% that works..."
**Why:** Flatters the viewer (you're about to be in the 1%) while creating urgency.
**Example:** "99% of founders get their first hire wrong. Here's the 1% that works..."

### 10. The Myth Buster
**Formula:** "The biggest myth in [industry]: [common belief]. The truth is..."
**Why:** Positions speaker as authority who sees through noise.
**Example:** "The biggest myth in real estate: you need money to get started. The truth is..."

## Category 3: RELATABILITY (The "that's so me" effect drives shares)

### 11. The "POV"
**Formula:** "POV: You just [relatable situation everyone has experienced]"
**Why:** Instant identification. Viewer sees themselves.
**Example:** "POV: You just realized your content strategy is just posting and praying"

### 12. The "If You're [Type]"
**Formula:** "If you're [specific type of person], this is for you..."
**Why:** Self-selection creates commitment. If they identify, they watch.
**Example:** "If you're a creator making great content but getting zero engagement, this is for you..."

### 13. The Confession
**Formula:** "I'll be honest — I [vulnerable admission] for [time period]..."
**Why:** Vulnerability creates trust. Imperfection is relatable.
**Example:** "I'll be honest — I had no idea what I was doing for the first two years of my business..."

### 14. The "Before I Knew"
**Formula:** "Before I learned [thing], I was [embarrassing/common mistake]..."
**Why:** Promises transformation. Viewer wants the "after."
**Example:** "Before I learned hook formulas, I was losing viewers in the first second of every video..."

### 15. The Struggle Acknowledgment
**Formula:** "Can we talk about how hard [specific thing] actually is?"
**Why:** Validates the viewer's struggle. Creates community through shared difficulty.
**Example:** "Can we talk about how hard it actually is to stay consistent with content?"

## Category 4: VALUE / TACTICAL (Save-worthy content = algorithmic gold)

### 16. The Framework
**Formula:** "The [name] framework for [desired outcome]: [step 1, step 2, step 3]"
**Why:** Named frameworks feel proprietary and save-worthy.
**Example:** "The 3-2-1 framework for content that converts: 3 hooks, 2 stories, 1 CTA"

### 17. The "Here's Exactly How"
**Formula:** "Here's exactly how I [achieved specific result] in [timeframe]..."
**Why:** "Exactly" promises no fluff. Specificity promises actionability.
**Example:** "Here's exactly how I grew from 0 to 10K followers in 90 days..."

### 18. The Cheat Code
**Formula:** "The [topic] cheat code nobody is using..."
**Why:** Implies unfair advantage. FOMO if they don't watch.
**Example:** "The LinkedIn cheat code nobody is using to get 10x more reach..."

### 19. The Mistake List
**Formula:** "[Number] mistakes I made [doing thing] so you don't have to"
**Why:** Learning from others' mistakes feels efficient. Save-worthy.
**Example:** "5 mistakes I made pricing my services so you don't have to"

### 20. The Template/Swipe
**Formula:** "Steal this [template/script/formula] for [desired outcome]..."
**Why:** "Steal" implies high value being given away. Instant save.
**Example:** "Steal this cold DM template that gets 40% response rates..."

## Category 5: STORY / NARRATIVE (Stories bypass skepticism)

### 21. The Origin Moment
**Formula:** "The moment I realized [everything changed/I was wrong/the truth]..."
**Why:** Promises a turning point revelation.
**Example:** "The moment I realized my business model was built on a lie..."

### 22. The Client Story
**Formula:** "One of my [clients/students] came to me with [problem]. Here's what happened..."
**Why:** Social proof through narrative. Viewer projects themselves as the client.
**Example:** "One of my clients came to me posting 3x a week with zero leads. Here's what happened next..."

### 23. The "Last Week" Story
**Formula:** "Last [week/month], something happened that completely changed how I [thing]..."
**Why:** Recency creates relevance. "This just happened" feels urgent.
**Example:** "Last week, something happened that completely changed how I think about pricing..."

### 24. The Worst Day
**Formula:** "The worst day of my [career/business/life] taught me [lesson]..."
**Why:** Tension + resolution. Vulnerability + wisdom.
**Example:** "The worst day of my business taught me the most important lesson about leadership..."

### 25. The Conversation
**Formula:** "Someone asked me [question] yesterday. My answer surprised them..."
**Why:** Dialogue format is naturally engaging. The surprise creates a hook within a hook.
**Example:** "Someone asked me how much I charge yesterday. My answer surprised them..."

## Category 6: AUTHORITY / PROOF (Results speak)

### 26. The Results Reveal
**Formula:** "I [did specific thing] for [timeframe]. Here are the actual results..."
**Why:** Transparency + specificity = trust.
**Example:** "I posted every day for 90 days. Here are the actual results..."

### 27. The "What I'd Do Different"
**Formula:** "If I had to start [thing] over from zero, here's exactly what I'd do..."
**Why:** Implies mastery. "Over from zero" makes it accessible to beginners.
**Example:** "If I had to start my agency over from zero, here's exactly what I'd do..."

### 28. The Behind-the-Scenes
**Formula:** "Here's what [process/day/deal] actually looks like..."
**Why:** Peek behind the curtain. Authenticity signal.
**Example:** "Here's what a $50K deal actually looks like from start to finish..."

### 29. The Tool/Stack Reveal
**Formula:** "The exact [tools/systems/stack] I use to [outcome]..."
**Why:** Actionable, save-worthy, and creates reciprocity.
**Example:** "The exact tools I use to create 30 days of content in 2 hours..."

### 30. The Income/Metric Breakdown
**Formula:** "Here's how I [made/grew/saved] [specific number] in [timeframe]..."
**Why:** Numbers are concrete. Transparent metrics build trust.
**Example:** "Here's how I went from $2K to $15K/month in 6 months as a solo creator..."

## Category 7: URGENCY / TIMELINESS (Act now or miss out)

### 31. The Algorithm Update
**Formula:** "[Platform] just changed [thing]. Here's what it means for you..."
**Why:** Urgency + relevance. Feels like breaking news.
**Example:** "Instagram just changed how Reels are ranked. Here's what it means for your reach..."

### 32. The Window Closing
**Formula:** "There's a [window/opportunity] right now that won't last..."
**Why:** Scarcity creates action. FOMO is real.
**Example:** "There's a content format working right now on LinkedIn that won't last..."

### 33. The "Before It's Too Late"
**Formula:** "If you're not [doing thing] by [timeframe], you're going to [consequence]..."
**Why:** Loss aversion. Fear of falling behind.
**Example:** "If you're not using short-form video by Q2, you're going to lose 50% of your organic reach..."

## Category 8: QUESTION HOOKS (Engagement through curiosity)

### 34. The "Would You Rather"
**Formula:** "Would you rather [option A] or [option B]?"
**Why:** Forces mental engagement. People MUST pick a side.
**Example:** "Would you rather have 100K followers with no sales, or 1K followers buying everything you sell?"

### 35. The Diagnostic Question
**Formula:** "How do you know if [problem]? Watch for these [number] signs..."
**Why:** Self-assessment creates personal relevance.
**Example:** "How do you know if your content strategy is working? Watch for these 3 signs..."

### 36. The "What Would You Do"
**Formula:** "A client just [scenario]. What would you do?"
**Why:** Interactive. Invites comments. Creates debate.
**Example:** "A client just asked for a 50% discount on your highest package. What would you do?"

### 37. The Rhetorical Punch
**Formula:** "Why does everyone [common thing] when [better thing] exists?"
**Why:** Makes the viewer question their own behavior.
**Example:** "Why does everyone chase followers when they should be chasing saves?"

## Category 9: PATTERN INTERRUPTS (Stop the scroll physically)

### 38. The Cold Open
**Formula:** Start mid-sentence or mid-action. No intro. No context.
**Why:** Disorientation demands attention. The brain seeks context.
**Example:** "—and that's when he hung up the phone and I realized I'd been doing it wrong for 3 years."

### 39. The Contradiction
**Formula:** "[Do opposite of expected]. I'm serious."
**Why:** Cognitive dissonance. The brain needs resolution.
**Example:** "Delete your content calendar. I'm serious. Here's what to do instead..."

### 40. The Whisper/Lean-In
**Formula:** "Come closer. I need to tell you something most [people] won't..."
**Why:** Creates intimacy. Makes content feel exclusive.
**Example:** "Come closer. I need to tell you something most marketers won't admit..."

### 41. The List Interrupt
**Formula:** "[Thing 1], [Thing 2], [Thing 3]... and [unexpected/dark/funny thing]"
**Why:** Pattern establishment then break. The unexpected item hooks.
**Example:** "Coffee, laptop, quiet room... and the crippling fear that nobody will watch this."

## Category 10: TRANSFORMATION / ASPIRATION (The viewer wants to become)

### 42. The Before/After
**Formula:** "[Time period] ago I was [struggling state]. Now I [desired state]. Here's the pivot..."
**Why:** Proof of transformation + roadmap = maximum save-worthiness.
**Example:** "12 months ago I was posting into the void. Now I get 50 DMs a day from ideal clients. Here's the pivot..."

### 43. The Permission Slip
**Formula:** "You're allowed to [thing they feel guilty about]..."
**Why:** Removes shame. Creates emotional relief that builds trust.
**Example:** "You're allowed to charge more than your competitors. Here's why..."

### 44. The Identity Shift
**Formula:** "You're not a [limiting identity]. You're a [empowering identity] who [reframe]..."
**Why:** Identity-level content is the most shareable kind.
**Example:** "You're not a content creator. You're a media company who happens to be one person."

### 45. The Future Self
**Formula:** "In 12 months, you'll either [outcome A] or [outcome B]. The difference is [action]..."
**Why:** Forces visualization of two futures. Creates urgency.
**Example:** "In 12 months, you'll either have a content engine running on autopilot, or you'll still be staring at a blank caption box. The difference is the next 30 days."

## Category 12: META / SELF-AWARE HOOKS

### 49. The "This Will Flop"
**Formula:** "This [post/video] will probably flop but I need to say it anyway..."
**Why:** Reverse psychology. Low expectations create curiosity.
**Example:** "This video will probably flop but I need to say this: your content is too safe."

### 50. The Fourth Wall Break
**Formula:** "I know you're about to scroll past this, but [give them a reason to stay]..."
**Why:** Acknowledges their behavior. Creates meta-engagement.
**Example:** "I know you're about to scroll past this, but this one tip made me more money than my entire content strategy combined..."

### 51. The Anti-Hook
**Formula:** "No hook. No fancy intro. Just [raw truth/value]..."
**Why:** In a sea of hooks, authenticity IS the hook.
**Example:** "No hook. No fancy intro. Just the exact system I use to turn one podcast into 30 days of content."

### 52. The "Save This"
**Formula:** "Save this before [platform] buries it..."
**Why:** Urgency + implied value + trains the save behavior (algorithmic gold).
**Example:** "Save this before the algorithm buries it — the complete posting schedule for every platform in 2026."

## Usage Rules

1. **Never use the same hook formula twice in a row** on the same platform
2. **Match hook to platform**: Controversy works on X, Value works on LinkedIn, Relatability works on Instagram, Pattern Interrupts work on TikTok
3. **Test in batches**: Use 5 different formulas per week, track which ones your audience responds to
4. **Rotate categories**: Don't become "the hot take person" or "the list person" — variety prevents audience fatigue
5. **The 3-second rule**: If the hook doesn't land in 3 seconds of video or 1 line of text, it's not a hook — it's a warm-up. Cut the warm-up.
6. **Specificity multiplier**: "I grew my business" < "I went from $2K to $15K/month in 6 months." Numbers, timeframes, and details multiply hook power.
