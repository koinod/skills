# Deep Research — Quality Scorecard

Use this scorecard to evaluate any research output. Score each dimension 1-5. Minimum passing: 35/50.

## Scoring Dimensions

### 1. Scope Completeness (1-5)
- 5: All relevant sub-questions answered, nothing material missing
- 4: Most sub-questions answered, minor gaps noted
- 3: Core question answered but several sub-questions skipped
- 2: Partial answer, significant gaps
- 1: Superficial treatment, most sub-questions unanswered

### 2. Source Quality (1-5)
- 5: 80%+ Tier 1-2 sources, full CRAAP evaluation, diverse source types
- 4: Mix of Tier 1-3, most sources evaluated, reasonable diversity
- 3: Mostly Tier 3 sources, some evaluation, limited diversity
- 2: Tier 3-4 sources, minimal evaluation
- 1: Single source type, no evaluation, or mostly Tier 4-5

### 3. Triangulation (1-5)
- 5: Every material claim corroborated by 3+ independent sources
- 4: Most claims corroborated by 2+ sources, gaps flagged
- 3: Some triangulation, several single-source claims unflagged
- 2: Minimal triangulation, mostly single-source
- 1: No triangulation, sources not cross-referenced

### 4. Contrarian Analysis (1-5)
- 5: Substantive counter-thesis with evidence, stress-tested conclusions
- 4: Clear counter-arguments identified and addressed
- 3: Token contrarian section, not deeply explored
- 2: Mentioned opposing views without engagement
- 1: No contrarian analysis, one-sided presentation

### 5. Confidence Scoring (1-5)
- 5: Every finding tagged, tags are accurate and calibrated
- 4: Most findings tagged, generally accurate
- 3: Some findings tagged, calibration inconsistent
- 2: Few tags, or tags seem arbitrary
- 1: No confidence scoring

### 6. Blind Spot Honesty (1-5)
- 5: Specific unknowns identified, explains why they matter, suggests how to resolve
- 4: Unknowns identified with context
- 3: Generic "more research needed" statements
- 2: Brief mention of limitations
- 1: No acknowledgment of gaps or unknowns

### 7. Temporal Depth (1-5)
- 5: Full timeline, inflection points identified, trajectory projected
- 4: Key historical context, some trend analysis
- 3: Brief historical mention
- 2: Current snapshot only, no temporal context
- 1: No temporal dimension

### 8. Actionability (1-5)
- 5: Specific actions, prioritized, with effort/value estimates, tailored to requester
- 4: Clear actions with priorities
- 3: Generic recommendations
- 2: Vague suggestions
- 1: No actionable output, just information dump

### 9. Synthesis Quality (1-5)
- 5: Original insight extracted from data patterns, not just summarized
- 4: Clear patterns identified and explained
- 3: Organized summary but limited insight extraction
- 2: List of facts without connection
- 1: Copy-paste from sources, no synthesis

### 10. Methodology Transparency (1-5)
- 5: Full process documented, limitations stated, reproducible
- 4: Process mostly documented, key limitations noted
- 3: Brief methodology mention
- 2: Implied but not stated methodology
- 1: No methodology documentation

## Grades
- **45-50**: Institutional grade. Would satisfy a hedge fund PM or McKinsey partner.
- **35-44**: Professional grade. Solid basis for business decisions.
- **25-34**: Adequate. Useful but has gaps that could lead to blind spots.
- **15-24**: Weak. Not sufficient for important decisions.
- **Below 15**: Reject. Glorified Google search.
