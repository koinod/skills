# Sales Methodology Reference

Embedded frameworks for scoring and coaching. The skill uses these to grade calls and generate methodology-specific feedback.

---

## SPIN Selling (Neil Rackham)

**Core premise**: Large sales are won through a sequence of questions that guide the prospect to articulate their own need, not through feature pitching or closing techniques.

### The Question Sequence

**S — Situation Questions**
- Gather facts about the prospect's current state
- Examples: "How many reps do you have?" / "What CRM are you using?" / "What does your current process look like?"
- **Scoring rule**: Fewer is better. Top performers ask 40% fewer situation questions than average. Each one costs you engagement.
- Red flag: More than 3 situation questions without a transition to problems

**P — Problem Questions**
- Explore difficulties, dissatisfactions, and challenges
- Examples: "Where does that process break down?" / "What happens when a rep misses quota?" / "What's the hardest part of your current approach?"
- **Scoring rule**: At least 2-3 problem questions should be asked. They should be specific to the prospect's context, not generic.
- Red flag: Jumping to solution after the first problem mentioned

**I — Implication Questions**
- Amplify the consequences of the problem. Make the pain bigger.
- Examples: "What does that cost you per quarter?" / "How does that affect your ability to hit target?" / "What happens to rep retention when that keeps occurring?"
- **Scoring rule**: This is where most reps fail. Top performers ask 2-4x more implication questions. This is the highest-correlation skill with close rates in complex sales.
- Red flag: Zero implication questions = the prospect has no urgency to act

**N — Need-Payoff Questions**
- Get the prospect to articulate the value of solving the problem
- Examples: "If you could cut that ramp time in half, what would that mean for Q3?" / "How would it help if your reps could see that data in real time?"
- **Scoring rule**: The prospect should be selling themselves by this point. If the rep is still pitching features, they skipped this step.
- Red flag: Rep answers their own need-payoff question instead of letting prospect talk

### SPIN Scoring Matrix (0-100)

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 90-100 | Full SPIN sequence executed, prospect articulated their own need, natural progression |
| 70-89 | Good problem/implication questions, minor sequence gaps |
| 50-69 | Some discovery but jumped to solution too early, weak implications |
| 30-49 | Mostly situation questions and feature pitching |
| 0-29 | No meaningful discovery, pure product dump |

## The Challenger Sale (Dixon & Adamson)

**Core premise**: The best sales reps don't just build relationships — they challenge the prospect's thinking with insights, tailor their message, and take control of the sale.

### Three Pillars

**Teach**
- Deliver a commercial insight the prospect didn't know
- Reframe how they think about their problem
- The insight must lead back to your unique capability (not generic advice)
- **Scoring rule**: Did the rep say something that made the prospect pause, rethink, or say "I never thought about it that way"?
- Examples of teaching: Industry data they haven't seen, cost-of-inaction math they haven't calculated, a pattern from similar companies
- Red flag: Rep shares insight that any competitor could also deliver (not differentiating)

**Tailor**
- Customize the message to the specific prospect's role, industry, and priorities
- Speak their language, reference their metrics, understand their pressures
- **Scoring rule**: Does the pitch feel like it was built for this person, or is it the same deck they show everyone?
- Red flag: Generic case studies from unrelated industries, one-size-fits-all ROI claims

**Take Control**
- Confidently guide the conversation and push back on deflections
- Discuss money before the prospect is "ready"
- Set clear next steps with specific dates and commitments
- **Scoring rule**: Did the rep lead the conversation or follow wherever the prospect wandered?
- Red flag: Rep agrees to "send over some info" without a booked next step

### Challenger Scoring Matrix (0-100)

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 90-100 | Delivered unique insight, tailored precisely, controlled pace and next steps |
| 70-89 | Good teaching moment but either generic tailoring or soft close |
| 50-69 | Some insight shared but no reframe, moderate control |
| 30-49 | Relationship-building focus, no challenging, weak control |
| 0-29 | Pure people-pleaser, no insight, no push, ended with "I'll follow up" |

## MEDDIC (Jack Napoli / PTC)

**Core premise**: Complex deals require rigorous qualification. If you can't fill in every letter, you don't have a real deal.

### The Six Elements

**M — Metrics**
- What quantifiable outcomes will the prospect achieve?
- Must be specific: "$400K saved" not "save money"
- **Scoring rule**: Were specific numbers discussed? Did the rep help the prospect calculate their own ROI?
- Red flag: Vague value claims with no math

**E — Economic Buyer**
- Who signs the check? Have they been engaged?
- **Scoring rule**: Has the rep confirmed who the economic buyer is? Have they met or has a plan been made to engage them?
- Red flag: Entire sales process with someone who can't say yes

**D — Decision Criteria**
- What factors will be used to evaluate options?
- Technical requirements, business outcomes, vendor reputation, price
- **Scoring rule**: Did the rep ask what criteria matter most? Did they position against those criteria?
- Red flag: Rep doesn't know what they're being evaluated on

**D — Decision Process**
- What are the steps between "interested" and "signed"?
- Timeline, approvals needed, legal review, procurement, pilot
- **Scoring rule**: Did the rep map the buying process with specific dates?
- Red flag: "They said they'll get back to us" with no timeline

**I — Identify Pain**
- What compelling problem drives urgency to act NOW?
- Must be pain the prospect feels personally, not just organizational
- **Scoring rule**: Did the prospect express genuine frustration/urgency, or are they just "exploring options"?
- Red flag: No pain = no urgency = no deal

**C — Champion**
- Who inside the organization is selling for you when you're not in the room?
- Must have influence AND personal motivation to see the deal close
- **Scoring rule**: Has the rep identified someone who will advocate internally? Have they enabled that person with materials/talking points?
- Red flag: No internal champion = you're hoping, not selling

### MEDDIC Scoring Matrix (0-100)

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 90-100 | All 6 elements confirmed with evidence |
| 70-89 | 4-5 elements confirmed, 1-2 in progress |
| 50-69 | 3 elements confirmed, others unknown |
| 30-49 | Only pain and some criteria, no process/buyer/champion work |
| 0-29 | Demo dump with no qualification |

## Sandler Selling System

**Core premise**: The sale is made in the prospect's head during discovery, not during the presentation. Establish an up-front contract, uncover pain before presenting, and never chase.

### Key Principles

**Up-Front Contract**
- Set expectations at the start: what we'll cover, how long, and what happens at the end (yes, no, or clear next step)
- **Scoring rule**: Did the rep set an agenda and get agreement? Did they define a clear outcome for the call?
- Example: "At the end of this, I'll either tell you we're not a fit, or we'll book a demo with your team. Either way, no pressure — sound fair?"

**Pain Funnel**
- Surface-level pain → specific impact → personal impact → emotional weight
- Move from "What's the problem?" to "How does that affect you personally?"
- **Scoring rule**: Did the rep go 3+ levels deep on pain, or accept the first answer?
- Red flag: "What keeps you up at night?" then immediately pitching

**Budget Discussion Before Proposal**
- Discuss investment range before building a proposal
- Sandler says: never present without knowing the budget
- **Scoring rule**: Was money discussed before a formal proposal/demo? Did the rep frame it as investment, not cost?
- Red flag: Full demo + proposal sent without ever discussing budget

**No Free Consulting**
- Don't give away the solution in the discovery call
- Create enough clarity that the prospect knows you understand their problem, without solving it for free
- **Scoring rule**: Did the rep hold back their solution until pain and budget were confirmed?

### Sandler Scoring Matrix (0-100)

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 90-100 | UFC set, deep pain funnel, budget confirmed, mutual decision made |
| 70-89 | Good pain discovery and budget discussion, weak UFC or close |
| 50-69 | Some pain uncovered, budget hinted at but not confirmed |
| 30-49 | No UFC, surface pain, jumped to pitch |
| 0-29 | Free consulting session, no qualification, ended with "let me send you info" |

| Deal Type | SPIN Weight | Challenger Weight | MEDDIC Weight | Sandler Weight |
|-----------|-------------|-------------------|---------------|----------------|
| Complex/Enterprise | 25% | 25% | 35% | 15% |
| Mid-Market SaaS | 30% | 30% | 20% | 20% |
| SMB/Transactional | 20% | 15% | 10% | 55% |
| Coaching/Consulting | 35% | 25% | 10% | 30% |
| Insurance | 25% | 10% | 15% | 50% |
| D2D | 15% | 10% | 5% | 70% |

The composite score gives the overall methodology grade. Individual scores highlight which framework the rep naturally gravitates toward and where they need development.
